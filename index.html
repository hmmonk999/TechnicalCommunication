Tech Comm Students

Ten students answered an anonymous survey about their experience at Iowa State as technical communication majors. The survey was intended to show the diversity in interests technical communication students have and show potential students what it is like to be a tech comm major at Iowa State.

Student A (Senior)

Major(s): Technical Communication

Minor(s): Digital Media

Why do you like technical communication?
I can be creative and analytical. It's a very open major so I can explore computer science, design, and English classes.

How did you hear about technical communication?
My aunt told me about technical writers when I was a junior in high school

What are your plans after graduation?
work for a few years as a designer then go to grad school in HCI

What is your dream job?
UX designer or multimedia specialist or a pirate

Student J (Freshman)

Major(s): Technical Communication

Minor(s): Digital Media

Why do you like technical communication?
It seems like a way to grow my technology skills and provide a reliable source of income while I pursue other passions

How did you hear about technical communication?
My advisor talked to me about it during my first visit to Iowa State as a possible branch for English majors to look into

What are your plans after graduation?
I'm still looking as of now. I intend to have my feelers out as I explore undergraduate research, internships, and career fairs to see where I should try to go.

What is your dream job?
I would love to design and write for zoos and other animal-related agencies, taking research and facts and turning it into something for the general public to consume. Someday, I would also love to become an established fiction writer as I could carry my love of reading and literature for the rest of my life, even once I retire from more hands-on work at zoos.

Path to Technical Communication

Many students start off with a different major. Seven of the ten students that were interviewed had a different major before technical communication. The Sankey diagram below shows how they got to technical communication. The white areas represent before the students got to Iowa State. Because not everyone started at the same time, the white areas end at different locations. The colorful part correlate with student colors above and the grey areas represent when the students change to technical communication up to the current semester.
